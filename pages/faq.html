  • Basics

  • What types of properties are auctioned on this site?

    We market bank-owned homes, foreclosures, and short sales on our site. It also promotes single/double family homes, and land.

  • Who supplies the property details?

    Generally the information for each property comes directly from the seller. In some instances public records are used in the property details, such as square footage and acreage. Property information provided is for reference only. It is the buyer’s responsibility to verify all property information, when they visit.

  • I am having trouble submitting a bid or offer online. What should I do?

    Please contact our Customer Care team via email at onlineauction@gmail.com or call us at (022)28991988.

  • Account

  • How do I register?

    Click “Register” in the upper right-hand corner of the page. Fill out the form and submit your information. Thereafter, you can use that information to log into the site.

  • Do I need to register to make a bid?

    Yes, it is absolutely necessary for us to have your details regitered. Click “Register” in the upper right-hand corner of the page. Fill out the form and submit your information. Thereafter, you can use that information to log into the site.

  • Do we have to pay up for using the site?

    Using the site is absolutely free, including the registration. You have to pay up only if you win a certain auction.

  • What is the method of Payment?

    We do not support any online payment methods. This is a place where the buyers meet sellers! So, we completely support the idea of you going there, seeing and inspecting the property, and then paying up by whatever means feasible to both the parties.

  • What is initial bid price?

    Initial bid is the price at which the auction starts. The offers are made above that price.

  • What is minimum bid price?

    A minimum or reserve price is the minimum price the seller is willing to accept for the property in an auction. The reserve price is set by the sellers and is not shown to buyers; however, the property details page does show if the reserve price has been met.

  • Auction

  • Does the sellr have to accept the highest offer?

    No, sellers can accept or reject any offer. They have the legal right to approve or reject offers based on all of the components of the offers, including financing type.

  • Can I withdraw my offer on an auction?

    Yes, you can withdrawn an offer if you want to, irrespective of the reasons. You have to go to the Auction page and the cancel your offer.

  • Why was my offer rejected?

    Offers are typically rejected for two reasons: 1. Another buyer submitted an offer with a higher price or more attractive terms, such as fewer concessions or a shorter closing timeframe. 2. The offer did not meet the seller’s approved terms.

  • How to know if the pictures put up by the seller are authentic?

    There is no way to know that for sure. That's why we encourage the sellers to go see the property once before buying.

  • Bid

  • How do you place a bid?

    While browsing through the properties, you can visit any of them and place your bid easily. Just press the place bid button on the properties page that opens up.

  • If I am the highest bidder, is it necessary that I'll get the property?

    No, it is not. the seller has every right to turn your offer down on any grounds.

  • How do I comes to know whether my bid is selected or not?

    The seller will contact you if your offer is accepted, via mail. You will even receive a PSA agrement with that

  • How long does it take to know if your bid is selected?

    A week or two from the day the auction was finalised. The seller can reject the bid if he does not find it legit.

  • Can the seller cancel an Auction?

    The seller can cancel the Auction if he wishes to. The seller might find an attractive offer or a good price for his property, and he would cancel the Auction in that case.

  • When is the purchase and sale agreement(PSA) signed?

    After you receive an email that your bid was the selected bid, we will email you with instructions on how to complete and submit the Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA). You must sign the PSA within 48 hours of receiving these instructions. Signatures on Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA), must be completed before acceptance is considered final.

  • What if I don't sign the PSA within 48hours?

    If you fail to sign and return the Purchase and Sale Agreement within 48 hours, the seller has the option to unilaterally cancel your offer or bid. Failing to return the PSA agreement in the time required may lead to indefinite suspension of your account.
